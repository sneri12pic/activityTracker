FocusTrace — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 17, 2026

FocusTrace is a local-first screen-time tracker. The short version: all of your data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share any data. The app makes no network connections.

Data the app stores (on your device only)

  • App usage sessions (which apps you used and for how long), recorded from Android usage statistics.
  • Your settings: app restrictions you configure, excluded apps, and language preference.

All of this is stored in a local database inside the app's private storage. It never leaves your device. There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics SDKs, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party data processors.

Permissions and why the app needs them

  • Usage access (PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS) — reading screen-time statistics is the app's core function. The data is processed and stored locally only.
  • Query installed apps (QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES) — used solely to show app names and icons for the apps you track or restrict, instead of raw package names.
  • Display over other apps — used to show the blocking overlay when an app restriction you configured is reached.
  • Notifications & foreground service — used to keep restriction enforcement running and to show its status.
  • Run at boot — restarts restriction enforcement after your device reboots.

Data deletion

You can erase everything at any time via Settings → Clear local data inside the app. Uninstalling the app also permanently deletes all stored data.

Children

FocusTrace is not directed at children under 13 and collects no data from anyone.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if an optional sync feature is added), the updated version will be published at this URL with a new effective date before the change ships.
